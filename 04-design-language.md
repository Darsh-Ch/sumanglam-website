# Design Language

Version: 1.0

Project: Sumanglam Digital Showroom Platform

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the visual, emotional, and experiential language of the Sumanglam website.

It acts as the single source of truth for:

- Design decisions
- UI consistency
- Content presentation
- AI-generated interfaces
- Future redesigns

Every page should feel like it belongs to the same ecosystem.

---

# 2. Core Brand Personality

Sumanglam should feel:

1. Luxury
2. Trustworthy
3. Warm
4. Modern
5. Sophisticated
6. Knowledgeable
7. Approachable

---

# 3. Brand Positioning

Sumanglam is:

A premium destination for kitchens, wardrobes, hardware, appliances, and interior solutions.

---

Sumanglam is NOT:

- A hardware store
- An ecommerce marketplace
- A discount retailer
- A technology startup
- A design agency

---

# 4. Emotional Design Goals

Visitors should feel:

"I trust these people."

"I can imagine my home looking like this."

"They understand quality."

"I should visit their showroom."

---

# 5. Visual Inspiration

Primary Influences:

- Apple
- Notion
- Premium Interior Studios
- Luxury Showrooms

---

Secondary Influences:

- Editorial Architecture Publications
- Premium Product Catalogs

---

Avoid copying:

- Ecommerce websites
- Template-heavy landing pages
- Generic corporate sites

---

# 6. Design Ratios

Visual Style Mix

Luxury Showroom        50%

Apple Minimalism       35%

Editorial Design       15%

---

# 7. Whitespace Philosophy

Whitespace is a feature.

Not empty space.

---

Rules

Use generous spacing.

Allow sections to breathe.

Avoid visual crowding.

Avoid dense layouts.

---

Users should feel calm while browsing.

---

# 8. Layout Philosophy

Content should feel curated.

Not stacked.

---

Prefer:

Large blocks

Strong hierarchy

Clear focal points

Intentional composition

---

Avoid:

Tiny cards everywhere

Overloaded grids

Visual clutter

---

# 9. Image Philosophy

Images are the primary storytelling tool.

---

Image Priority

1. Professional interiors
2. Kitchen environments
3. Wardrobes
4. Product close-ups
5. Showroom imagery

---

Every image should support aspiration.

---

# 10. Photography Style

Preferred

Natural lighting

Warm lighting

Architectural framing

Premium materials

Human-scaled spaces

---

Avoid

Flash photography

Over-saturated colors

Busy compositions

Low-quality mobile photography

Stock-photo aesthetics

---

# 11. Typography Philosophy

Typography should feel:

Elegant

Modern

Confident

Easy to read

---

Headlines

Short

Impactful

Minimal

---

Body Copy

Simple

Helpful

Human

Clear

---

# 12. Content Philosophy

Less text.

More meaning.

---

Prefer

Designed Around Your Home

---

Over

Long marketing paragraphs.

---

Every sentence should earn its place.

---

# 13. Navigation Philosophy

Navigation should feel effortless.

---

Users should never wonder:

"Where do I go next?"

---

Navigation should prioritize:

Exploration

Discovery

Inspiration

---

Not product catalogs.

---

# 14. Interaction Philosophy

Interactions should feel premium.

Not playful.

---

Default

Subtle

---

Allowed

Fade

Reveal

Parallax

Smooth transitions

---

Avoid

Bouncy animations

Attention-seeking motion

Aggressive effects

---

# 15. Component Philosophy

Every component should answer:

Does this increase trust?

Does this improve clarity?

Does this support inspiration?

---

If not, remove it.

---

# 16. Homepage Philosophy

The homepage exists to:

Inspire

Build trust

Encourage exploration

Drive showroom visits

---

It does NOT exist to:

Sell products immediately

Push consultations aggressively

Overwhelm users

---

# 17. Product Philosophy

Products support spaces.

Spaces do not support products.

---

Customer Flow

Space

↓

Inspiration

↓

Product

↓

Brand

↓

Consultation

---

Not

Product

↓

Specification

↓

Inquiry

---

# 18. Mobile Philosophy

Mobile is the primary experience.

---

Every feature should be designed for mobile first.

Desktop extends mobile.

Desktop does not define mobile.

---

# 19. Trust Signals

Trust should be communicated through:

Professional visuals

Premium brands

Showroom experience

Expert guidance

Clear information

---

Avoid artificial trust tactics.

No fake urgency.

No exaggerated claims.

No inflated statistics.

---

# 20. Design Anti-Patterns

Never Use

Glassmorphism

Neon colors

Cyberpunk aesthetics

Excessive gradients

Dark tech startup themes

Template-style layouts

Aggressive popups

Overcrowded sections

Auto-playing media

---

# 21. Success Test

Every page should pass this test:

If a first-time visitor lands here for 10 seconds, do they feel:

Luxury?

Trust?

Expertise?

Warmth?

Aspiration?

---

If not, the design should be revised.
